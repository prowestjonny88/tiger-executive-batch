# Theme 2: EV Charger Troubleshooting — Key Points

## 8. Theme 2 problem statement
EV charger downtime in Malaysia is caused by a mix of:
- technical faults
- connectivity issues
- simple operational mistakes such as:
  - Wi-Fi disconnections
  - chargers left offline
  - isolators turned off

Current troubleshooting is heavily manual and technician-dependent, which makes it:
- labour-intensive
- costly
- inefficient

The briefing explicitly says many issues could be resolved faster by:
- users
- local / on-site staff
if proper guidance existed.

## 9. Theme 2 core challenge
The missing capability is:
- intelligent, AI-driven inspection and diagnostic support that can:
  - identify the type of charger issue
  - determine whether it can be solved remotely
  - prioritize technician intervention only when necessary

The stated objective is to:
- streamline troubleshooting
- reduce unnecessary manual labour
- improve operational efficiency across EV charging networks

## 10. Existing infrastructure categories relevant to Theme 2
The briefing lists these relevant infrastructure categories:
1. AC & DC EV chargers
2. Charger LED indicator systems and display panels
3. Charging connectors and cables
4. Connectivity infrastructure
5. Company technicians
6. Maintenance and service operations

## 11. Theme 2 background / operational framing
The briefing stresses that:
- EV charger networks are growing rapidly with EV adoption
- downtime can come from:
  - wear and tear
  - faults
  - physical damage
  - overheating
  - misconfiguration
- manual troubleshooting is:
  - time-consuming
  - inconsistent
  - dependent on technician experience
- some issues can be resolved with guided assistance instead of technician dispatch

It also gives an external benchmark example from Teneo.ai, claiming AI-powered troubleshooting systems can:
- automate over 60% of interactions
- maintain up to 99% accuracy
- reduce operational costs by up to 50%
- handle troubleshooting, validation, and service scheduling at large scale

The briefing uses this to argue that AI-powered visual inspection and guided troubleshooting can:
- reduce labour dependency
- shorten downtime
- improve operational efficiency
- optimize technician deployment
- increase charging-network reliability

## 12. Theme 2 expected deliverables
A Theme 2 solution is expected to:
1. detect and classify charger faults
2. identify visual anomalies from images/videos
3. differentiate between simple / user-resolvable issues and faults requiring technician intervention
4. demonstrate classification accuracy

And each team must propose **one working prototype or proof-of-concept**.

## 13. What this means strategically for a Theme 2 team
The briefing strongly implies that a good Theme 2 submission should not stop at pure classification.
A strong solution should connect:
- visual inspection
- fault diagnosis
- routing / triage
- guided troubleshooting
- technician escalation when needed

The competition setup also means Theme 2 teams must design for:
- a working MVP early
- re-testing on new datasets during mentoring
- robustness to late-arriving final data
- a demo that clearly shows both technical accuracy and operational usefulness

## 14. Practical takeaways for building against the briefing
### Must respect
- working MVP is mandatory
- Round 2 and Round 3 introduce new data
- accuracy matters
- visual anomaly identification matters
- technician-vs-user resolution distinction matters

### Good product implications
- build for staged data updates
- keep fallback / uncertainty handling
- make demo and workflow clear, not just model-centric
- show business value through reduced unnecessary technician intervention
